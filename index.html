Manifesto

The Doctrine

I've spent over a decade designing AI systems for Regulated Industries — the kind where Architecture Decisions carry real consequences. Along the way, I noticed something missing from most AI education: a single, coherent place to learn everything from Mathematical Foundations to Multi-Agent Orchestration to the philosophy of Machine Cognition. So I'm building one.

This site is that attempt — a free, open, progressively structured Knowledge Architecture covering 1,000 foundational Research Papers, 1,117 Curated Resources across every domain I've found useful, a comprehensive curriculum from First Principles to Frontier Research, and a growing body of writing on the convergence of AI Systems Engineering and Consciousness Science. If we are building systems that reason alongside humans under uncertainty, the design constraints are not only computational. They are cognitive. This library reflects both sides.

Dossier

The Architect

AI architect by trade. I've spent the better part of fifteen years designing and deploying AI systems across Regulated Industries — Financial Services, Insurance, Payments, Risk — across Asia-Pacific and Europe. My work spans Multi-Agent Orchestration, LLM Architecture, Cloud Infrastructure, and the Governance Frameworks that make AI trustworthy enough for institutions that can't afford to get it wrong.

Consciousness researcher by calling. My independent research focuses on Human × Machine Intelligence — exploring how Artificial and Biological Cognition might be architecturally integrated rather than stacked in separate layers. My research draws on Computational Neuroscience, Phenomenology, and Contemplative Traditions including Kashmir Shaivism, Vedanta, and Abhidhamma.

Curriculum builder by necessity. When I started learning AI from First Principles, I couldn't find a single resource that went from "what is a number" to "how do you architect a Multi-Agent System for a regulated environment." So I started building one — and decided to share it openly. What you see on this site is that Ongoing Project.

I believe the best way to learn is to teach, and the best way to build expertise is to organize it for others. If anything here helps you move faster, that's the whole point.
